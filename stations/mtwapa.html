About ATDC Mtwapa

Established in 2007, ATDC Mtwapa is located in Kilifi County and serves the coastal region of Kenya. The center focuses on agricultural practices suited to the coastal climate, including coconut farming, cashew nut production, and tropical fruit cultivation.

Our station works closely with local farmers, agricultural extension officers, and stakeholders to promote sustainable farming practices, appropriate mechanization, and value addition for agricultural products.

With a team of 45 dedicated professionals, including agricultural engineers, food scientists, and technical experts, ATDC Mtwapa is committed to transforming coastal agriculture through innovation and knowledge transfer.

Our Specializations

Coastal Crop Production

Specialized training on coconut, cashew nuts, and tropical fruit production optimized for coastal conditions.

Value Addition

Processing techniques for coconut products, cashew nuts, and tropical fruits to increase market value.

SME Incubation

Supporting small and medium enterprises in the agricultural sector through training and technology access.

Our Facilities

  • Modern training center with capacity for 100 participants
  • Food processing workshop for value addition training
  • Agricultural machinery workshop and demonstration area
  • 15-acre demonstration farm for practical training
  • Soil testing laboratory for agricultural advice
  • SME incubation center for agricultural entrepreneurs
  • Modern irrigation systems for demonstration
  • Conference facilities for agricultural events

Success Stories

Coconut Value Chain Development: ATDC Mtwapa has trained over 500 farmers in coconut value addition, leading to the establishment of 20 small-scale coconut processing enterprises in Kilifi and Kwale counties. These enterprises now produce coconut oil, coconut flour, and coconut fiber products that are sold locally and in tourist markets along the coast.

SME Incubation Program: Through our SME incubation program, we have supported 15 agricultural enterprises, providing them with technical training, business mentorship, and access to processing equipment. One notable success is "Coastal Delights," a company that processes tropical fruits into jams and preserves, now employing 30 local youth.

Climate-Smart Agriculture Initiative: In response to changing weather patterns along the coast, ATDC Mtwapa has trained 300 farmers in climate-smart agricultural practices. Participating farmers have reported a 40% increase in crop resilience during drought periods and a 25% increase in overall yields.
